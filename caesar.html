Caesar Cipher
In Cryptography, a Caesar Cipher is a substitution method where letters in the alphabets are replaced with the letter with a number of positions down the alphabet according to the key provided.
Example - Shifting the letter 'A' with a key of 2 would output the letter 'C'.
Usage
To encrypt the plaintext into ciphertext, enter the text you want to encrypt in the input text box, pick the key you want, and press the 'Encryt' button.
